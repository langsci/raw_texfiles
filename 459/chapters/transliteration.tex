\footnotetext{Georgian names in citations and running text are transliterated in the following way. Exceptions are those authors that already have transliterated their names according to the German or French spelling system (e.g. Tschenkéli, not Chkhenkeli; Charachidzé, not Sharashidze). The characters \textit{ej, j, vi, q, \={o}} are only used in Old Georgian, and are not used in names.}
