\chapter{Noun dictionary} \label{ch:noun dictionary}
\section{Basic nouns ending in \stem{-aˀ} {\nounstemformer}, \stem{-tr-aˀ} {\nominalizer-\nounstemformer} or \stem{-hsr-aˀ} {\nominalizer-\nounstemformer}} \label{ch:basic nouns ending in [-aˀ], [-tr-aˀ] or [-hsr-aˀ]}
The following basic nouns all end with \stem{-aˀ} {\nounstemformer}. Some of these nouns also have a \textsc{nominalizer} suffix, and end either with  \stem{-tr-aˀ} \textsc{\nominalizer-\nsf} or \stem{-(h)sr-aˀ} \textsc{\nominalizer-\nsf} (for which, see \sectref{ch:Basic nouns}). Basic nouns begin with \stem{ga-} \textsc{3s.a}, \stem{o-} \textsc{3s.p} or [a] (see \sectref{ch:Basic nouns}).
