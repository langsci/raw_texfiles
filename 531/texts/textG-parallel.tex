    \ParallelLText{\noindent \textit{dastanû î hewramanû êmeyçe duwê padşε eçê ba. yoşa be namêw sibhan aẍeywe. yoşa be namêw cemşêr şaywe. cemşêr şa ce dûrew aman. aman gerekiş bîyen î memliketî dagîr kero pey wêş. haɫîta? aman êgehe. eçê beynê nûrûyîş bîyen. penca şes neferê şûnîşo bîyênêw. aman luwan a qeɫaw hewramanî e pagewe nîştenre.}}
    \ParallelRText{\noindent As for the tales of this Hewraman region of ours, there used to be two kings here. One of them was called Sibhan Agha. One of them was called Jamsher Shah. Jamsher Shah came to Hewraman from far away. He wanted to occupy this region and control it in his own interest. You see? He came here. He had forces here. Some fifty, sixty persons backed him. He came here and went to the citadel of Hewraman over there and settled there.}
\end{Parallel}

    \vspace{.4cm} 

\begin{Parallel}{0.47\textwidth}{0.47\textwidth}
    \ParallelLText{\noindent \textit{haminiş aman milre. ħewt saɫê pawe bîyen. a wextî î cemşêr şa, sibhan aẍey çew deso bîyen. dawaw aẍeyîş kerdêne. î memlikete desû ađîwe bîyen. ađ padşaş bîyen. ta padşa nebîyen ađ padşaş bîyen. a wextîyekey îđîç aman daw aw padşagerîş kerdênew. gerekiş bîyen hewramanî dawa kero, dagîr kero.}}
    \ParallelRText{\noindent Then, it became summer. He was up there for seven years. Jamsher Shah, eh … Sibhan Agha was on the other side of the village. Jamsher Shah claimed governorship over Hewraman. This region was under Sibhan Agha's control: He was the king. Sibhan Agha was its king until there were no kings. Then, Jamsher Shah came and claimed kingship. He wanted to occupy Hewraman.}
\end{Parallel}

    \vspace{.4cm}

\begin{Parallel}{0.47\textwidth}{0.47\textwidth}
    \ParallelLText{\noindent \textit{sibhan aẍey neyan deso. wateniş, ``min zîndigîyû babiɫbabîm eçê bîyen. î baxû baxatû hew\-rama\-nî, memliketû hew\-rama\-nî, xeɫkû hew\-rama\-nî a wextîyekey ême hukimřewiş bîyênmê. ême, min îney međew desû towe. min îney dew desû towe ey wêm kowe bilû? to luwew çiko amanî. luwewewe!''}}
    \ParallelRText{\noindent Sibhan Agha did not hand over the governorship to him, saying, ``I, my ancestors, have all lived here. We have ruled over these gardens of Hewraman, this region of Hewraman, and this people of Hewraman. I will not hand these over to you. If I hand over these to you, where should I go myself? Go back to where you came from. Go back!''}
\end{Parallel}

    \vspace{.4cm} 

\begin{Parallel}{0.47\textwidth}{0.47\textwidth}
    \ParallelLText{\noindent \textit{şûnû aneyre beynê eça bîyenû. sibhan aẍey a wextîyekey aẍe bîyen ħîn bîyen sibhan aẍe ça aẍegerîş kerdenû. î maɫû miɫke hinû ađî bîyenû. desşo bîyenû. xeɫk luwan temenaş pey gêrtenû. şûnû aneyre wextê zano hamin amaw gîriş kerd sibhan aẍe milo awekêw cemşêr aẍey biřo, hesaryewe.}}
    \ParallelRText{\noindent Jamsher Shah was still in the citadel. Sibhan Agha was the thingummy, the ruler. Sibhan Agha governed over there. These lands and properties here were his. They were under his control. People went and pleaded with him not to hand over power. Then, as Sibhan Agha knew summer was coming and Jamsher Agha was stuck, Sibhan Agha went and cut off Jamsher Shah's water supply using a wall.}
\end{Parallel}

    \vspace{.4cm} 

\begin{Parallel}{0.47\textwidth}{0.47\textwidth}
    \ParallelLText{\noindent \textit{be guncêşa ardêne. ahekû guncêw ar\-dê\-neşa ew dîmû qeɫε. eça awekêş biřo. awekê biřo. milonû wer werwe maro be nîrûwe\-keyş de koɫê penc koɫê gi řowê îzafeyş wizo pa sextre maçaş qeɫaw hewramanî.}}
    \ParallelRText{\noindent Jamsher Shah's men supplied water through earthenware pipes. They delivered the water to the other side of the citadel using earthenware pipes. There, Sibhan Agha cut off Jamsher Shah's water supply. He cut off the water supply. Jamsher Shah fetched snow, five or ten loads daily, using his men. He would throw whatever was left of the snow at that hard-to-access mountain called the citadel of Hewraman.}
\end{Parallel}

    \vspace{.4cm}

\begin{Parallel}{0.47\textwidth}{0.47\textwidth}
    \ParallelLText{\noindent \textit{wextê werwekê kem pa kotay maro pey kemî milo hurgêro hurmêzo hur çolekêwe gêro minyo baxeɫêş. be xatirû îney qesem wero qesemiş negino. wateniş, ``min ta diɫî î gîyane îna qefesû sîney minne diɫî beyim çađî nîya.'' ``bê ba nîşmêre seɫahû wêma kermê.''}}
    \ParallelRText{\noindent When the little snow that was left was about to disappear from the mountains, Jamsher Shah rose and grabbed a sparrow and put it on his chest under his clothing. He wanted to swear an oath using the sparrow. He did not want to swear falsely on himself. He said, ``I swear on the soul that is in my chest, I am not angry with Sibhan Agha.'' He said to Sibhan Agha, ``Let us sit together and reach a solution.''}
\end{Parallel}

    \vspace{.4cm}

\begin{Parallel}{0.47\textwidth}{0.47\textwidth}
    \ParallelLText{\noindent \textit{ewîç nimê maço, ``nexeyr nimewnew mitmanim nîya penet. desgîrim kero ađ nûrîş firen.'' maço, ``ne bê!'' ``luwe waçe bê!'' wat, ``bê hem mêman bo nîmeřo hem ba qisê kermê. ađ karû wêşû min hinû wêm.''}}
    \ParallelRText{\noindent Sibhan Agha did not go to Jamsher Shah, saying, ``No, I won't come to you; I don't trust you. He is going to arrest me. He has many men.'' Jamsher Shah said, ``No I won't arrest you. Come!'' He said to his men, ``Go and tell him to come!'' He said, ``May he come and be our guest for lunch, and we will discuss the matter. Then, he will go on his way, and I will go on mine.''}
\end{Parallel}

\begin{Parallel}{0.47\textwidth}{0.47\textwidth}
    \ParallelLText{\noindent \textit{a wextîyekey wextê mênê a wextî herçê qisê kera, herçê behsê kera herçê hesabê kera, qêxaw hewramanî herçê ba a wextî isɫahşa mebo. isɫahşa mebonew. nemo çolekekê weriş miđo. maço, ``dey min îney pîneym waten. î gîyanû qefesew sîney minne înen ađe luwa. anen mirîçêɫêm wer dan, to xeyrû min siɫa\-met.'' ``da dey dewrşa gêrdê ba deẍaɫet kerû.'' dewrşa gêra deẍaɫet kero.}}
    \ParallelRText{\noindent Then, Sibhan Agha and his men came to Jamsher Shah. No matter how much the headmen of Hewraman talked to both of them, discussed the issue, and tried to mediate, Jamsher Shah and Sibhan Agha disagreed. They disagreed. Jamsher Shah took the sparrow and let it go. Jamsher Shah said to Sibhan Agha, ``When I swore an oath, I swore on this sparrow. The soul in my rib cage that I swore an oath on was this sparrow. It just flew away. I have let the bird go away. Now, you follow your life, and I will follow mine!'' Jamsher Shah said to his men, ``Encircle them so that I can defeat Sibhan Agha.'' They encircled them and he defeated Sibhan Agha.}
\end{Parallel}
