\chapter{Verb extensions} %\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Appendix I: Verb extensions}
\label{sec:AppendixI}
% \chaptermark{Appendix I: Verb extensions}

%\clearpage
% \pagestyle{plain}

In this appendix, I provide the different extension forms for each verb in the verb database. In some cases, certain extension forms yield a semantic shift or a meaning different than expected.  These can be found in the lexicon in \appref{sec:AppendixIII}, while this appendix on verb extensions just lists existing forms.

As a notational convention, I do not indicate morpheme breaks when they are opaque. This is, for instance, the case with some passive forms of trisyllabic verbs where the passive -{\itshape a} also affects the penultimate vowel of the second syllable, as in {\itshape bùmɛlɛ} `hit sth.', which has a passive form {\itshape bùmala} `be hit' instead of *{\itshape bùmɛl-a}. Some verbs clearly have an extension morpheme, but lack a synchronic underived form, as discussed in \sectref{sec:VDeriv}. In these cases, I list the verb with its extension morpheme as a basic verb and mark the extension morpheme in bold. Finally, syllables that do not have any tonal marking are underlyingly toneless (\sectref{sec:Tinventory}); only the verb root is lexically specified for tone.
