\footnote{
		The term \ve{\?otos} has no known independent meaning in Amarasi outside of this nominal phrase.
		Regadring the place name \ve{Koor\?oot}, this is the form used in Amarasi.
		In Indonesian/Kupang Malay speakers usually unmetathesise both the final
		element \ve{\?oot} {\ra} \ve{\?oto} and the initial element \ve{koor} {\ra} \ve{koro}.
		(The initial element is in the M-form due to the following attributive phrase, see \S\ref{sec:AttMod}).
		Thus, in Indonesian the hamlet of \ve{Koor\?oot} is known as \it{Koro{\Q}oto}.}
