The full NP in this sentence is \textit{yome ene ati}, and it consists of a masculine noun followed by two inalienably possessed nouns. \textit{Yome} is the possessor of \textit{ene}, and \textit{yome ene} is the possessor of \textit{ati}. That is, the NP means the voices of the ones affected by the jaguar, i.e. the peccaries. In (\ref{ex:20-a12}) \textit{yama} is used instead of \textit{yome} to refer to the jaguar, and since \textit{yama} is feminine, the feminine \textit{ihi} is used instead of the masculine \textit{ehene}; but the meaning is the same.}
