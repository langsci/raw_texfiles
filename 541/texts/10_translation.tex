\begin{Parallel}{0.47\textwidth}{0.47\textwidth}
    \ParallelLText{\textit{Ttongo toto we yäbäd a angde goklanän a särem a deyawinän, wel ulle da gongkamän pädoenen de kämag ma alle. Ttängäm a säremang abal gogän. Auseda bo llɨg kälsre da ngänaeka de gongkamän. Ause da känyerkänyer däganän. Ause da bogo daeya llɨg eka täp me gognän a ine ulle da bogo ade gongällbänmällnän a tater ingoll ngädnan ngädnan deyarnän. Oblle ddone abal mulldae gogän dinduma ngalen a adawatta däbe daeya bem ulle amne me kui daeya. Ine da angde kui de mänddmändd dängkamän, ause da llɨg kälsre de käg mamon att e däzanän a dainttemänggän. Ause da kollba we gopnaeyän a ine ik mi ikopmeny gogän.}}
    \ParallelRText{One evening, when the sun had set and darkness had settled, a big wind started to blow from the west. The village became very dark. The old woman’s little boy started to cry. The old woman soothed him. The old woman was interrupting the boy when a big wave rose up and came rolling in like a mat. She didn’t have any way to run because this island was in the center of the sea. When the water began to drown the island, the old woman put the small boy in a shaped container and left him to float. The old woman turned into a fish and disappeared into the water.}
\end{Parallel}

\begin{Parallel}{0.47\textwidth}{0.47\textwidth}
    \ParallelLText{\textit{Llɨg kälsre de ine da enanae dae dätramän do ddob llayaba pate dängttägän. Ttongo llɨgmeny mik da oblle damändän a obo patme ulle gogän. Ge llɨg a eraeya ddobae mängallang daeya ako ddobae melem ngasngesang daeya ada ai lla ulle a tupi daeya. Ge llɨg a dedme gunziwän obo mälla da tämamae apte ttang llɨpɨt.}}
    \ParallelRText{The water carried the small boy straight through and he arrived near some people. A childless widow raised him and under her he became big. This boy was really very strong and a good worker. He was good, big, and tall. This boy immigrated there and his wives numbered five.}
\end{Parallel}
